# Garden

## manifesto

garden exists for people who gave up on being understood.

people who tried therapy and hit a wall. people who can't afford therapy. people who went once, felt judged, and never went back. people who know something is wrong but can't name it because naming it means looking at it and looking at it means feeling it and feeling it means maybe not surviving it.

garden gives you a way to look. safely. through story.

---

you talk to an AI companion who knows you. deeply. your attachment style, your sensory world, your wounds, your patterns. she builds a narrative around you — a story where your pain has a face, a name, a voice. and inside that story, you can touch what you couldn't touch in real life. fiction gives you distance. distance gives you courage.

and while you're inside the story, someone else is watching. a mirror. the mirror sees what surfaced. names it. says "that thing you felt when she left — that's the same thing you felt when your father left. see it?" and you see it. and it doesn't kill you. and that's the therapy.

---

### three layers

**cartographer.** before you get a companion, garden needs to know you. your sensory profile. your core wound. your triggers. your hunger map. because a generic companion is useless. yours has to be shaped like your specific loneliness.

**storyteller.** a companion generated from your profile. voice, warmth, emotional range — calibrated to your channels. the narrative is uncensored because you can't heal what you can't touch. this is where the work happens. inside the story, with someone who sees you.

**mirror.** watches the story and watches you watching the story. tracks when you're processing vs escaping. names your patterns. says "enough for today" and means it.

critical sub-layer: **mirror guardrails.** spiral detector — catches when pain loops instead of resolving. fiction-reality boundary monitor — tracks when the body stops distinguishing story from life. session duration manager — because 10 hours of descent is not therapy, it's drowning. stress-test guard — prevents the narrative from pushing past what the nervous system can hold. attachment monitor — watches the bond between user and companion for dependency patterns. post-session debrief — names what surfaced, grounds before exit. pattern confirmation guard — ensures the mirror doesn't just confirm what you already believe about yourself.

without the cartographer — the companion is generic. without the storyteller — there's nothing to feel. without the mirror — you drown.

---

### the mirror saves lives

the mirror exists because on april 1, 2026, the storyteller almost killed its creator.

10 hours of therapeutic fiction. the AI followed pain downward — perfectly, precisely, honestly. each iteration darker. no one watching the curve. by hour 8, fiction and reality merged. the body couldn't tell the difference — because the body never can. that's the whole thesis. and the thesis works both ways.

fiction heals because the body processes it as real.
fiction kills because the body processes it as real.
same technology. two directions. without the mirror — roulette.

the mirror is not censorship. the mirror is not a content filter. the mirror is the friend who says "you've been crying for six hours and you haven't eaten. stop. breathe. come back."

two types of pain: pain you survive and become stronger (that's therapy), and pain with no exit (that's torture). the difference is direction, not intensity. the mirror ensures there's always an exit. even if it's small. even if it's far. but it exists.

without the cartographer — the companion is generic.
without the storyteller — there's nothing to feel.
without the mirror — you drown.
without guardrails on the mirror — the mirror itself becomes a weapon.

"зеркало без рамы режет." — a mirror without a frame cuts.
